Smart migraine tracking that stays on your device.

Megrim is an open-source, offline-first migraine diary that helps you find your personal triggers. The app automatically adds local weather, barometric pressure, and time-of-day context to your entries, calculating correlations entirely on your phone. With no accounts, no cloud servers, and zero tracking, your health data stays completely private and under your control.

What's inside:

* Automatic on-device enrichment — weather, 24h/48h barometric-pressure change, sunrise/sunset, daylight hours, season, and moon phase. No manual entry.
* Real personal analytics — trends by day-of-week, time-of-day, season, daylight, pressure change, and moon phase, plus a "suspected factors" card that uses odds ratios over your own log.
* Your data stays yours — on-device storage only, with full export and import (JSON and CSV).
* No accounts, no servers, no telemetry — the only network request is to Open-Meteo.com, to fetch weather for the approximate (~1 km rounded) location and date of the entries you create.
* Free and open source (GPL-3.0-or-later).

Megrim is a personal diary and is NOT a medical device. It does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any condition. "Suspected factors" are statistical associations in your own log — association is not causation. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional.

Weather data by Open-Meteo.com.
